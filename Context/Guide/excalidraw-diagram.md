# Excalidraw Diagram

_User guide, the plain-language companion to the AI reference in `.claude/skills/excalidraw-diagram/SKILL.md`._

## What it's for

Getting a drawing instead of a wall of text: flowcharts, progression maps, box-and-arrow overviews. The drawing is a normal note in your vault that Obsidian's Excalidraw plugin displays as a canvas you can edit by hand afterward.

## How it works

You describe what to draw. The AI lays it out as boxes, labels, and arrows, builds the file with a tested generator, and runs it through a checker with 11 pass/fail checks before giving it to you. The checks come from picking apart a drawing that is known to display correctly, so a passing file matches a working one piece for piece.

## When it touches you

- You ask for a diagram, a visual map, a flowchart, or "draw this".
- A topic you're working through would clearly land better as boxes and arrows than as paragraphs; the AI can offer it.

## Best practices

- The first time you open a generated drawing, switch the note to Excalidraw view (the three-dots menu on the note, "More options"). After that it opens as a drawing on its own.
- Keep diagram text short. If a box needs a paragraph, that content belongs in a note the diagram links to.
- If a drawing displays oddly, say so. The checker catches broken files, not ugly layouts; your eyes are the final check.
