## The folders

Two files at the top level are the AI's instructions: **`main.md`** (your rules and preferences, the one you filled in) and **`CLAUDE.md`** (a short pointer that sends Claude to `main.md`).

The folders each have a job:

| Folder | What lives here |
|---|---|
| `Context/` | The AI's brain: its skills, memory, history, and the maps that tie them together |
| `Workshop-Human/` | Your inbox. Jot messy ideas here and point the AI at them |
| `Projects/` | A note per project you're working on |
| `Input/` | Drop files here for the AI to process |
| `Resources/` | Reference material you want to keep |
| `Attachments/` | Images and files embedded inside your notes |
| `AI-Workshop/` | The AI's own working area: its tools, outputs, and scheduled jobs |

**You don't have to memorize this.** Every folder contains a file named `HUMAN.md` that explains what the folder is for. Open any folder, read its `HUMAN.md`, and you'll know what belongs in it.

## Links between notes

Type `[[` in any note and Obsidian offers to link to another note, giving you something like `[[Home]]`. Click a link and you jump there. These links are the heart of Obsidian: they connect related notes so a train of thought stays followable months later. The AI creates them too when it writes.

## Backlinks: the same connection, seen from the other side

If this note links to `[[Home]]`, then the Home note lists *this* note as a **backlink**: a note pointing back at it.

Why that's useful: you never have to remember what connects to what. Open any note, open the right sidebar, and the Backlinks panel shows every note that mentions or links to it. Connections you made months ago resurface on their own.

Official references:

- Internal links: https://help.obsidian.md/links
- Backlinks: https://help.obsidian.md/plugins/backlinks
