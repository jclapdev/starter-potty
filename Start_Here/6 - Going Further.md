You're set up and you've run a session. These are the habits and places that make the system compound.

## The inbox habit

Anytime you have an idea, half-thought, or pasted link, drop it in the `Workshop-Human/` folder and tell the AI to process it. You write messy; it turns the mess into real work. (That folder's `HUMAN.md` has the details.)

## Turn repeated tasks into skills

When you notice you're asking for the same kind of thing again and again, tell the AI: *"I want to turn this into a skill."* It builds a reusable process and saves it, so the task gets easier and more consistent every time after.

## The session rhythm

- **Start** with `read your instructions`: the AI reads its handoff notes and tells you what's open.
- **Work** by describing what you want. The AI picks the right skill; you don't name files or steps.
- **End** with `wrap up`: it files what happened so next time starts warm.

Skipping wrap-up doesn't break anything, but the handoff is what carries context across sessions; make it a habit.

## Where to look things up later

This onboarding gets you started once. After that:

- **`HUMAN.md`** (vault root): the whole operation on one page, including troubleshooting and updating.
- **The Guide** (`Context/Guide/`): one plain page per mechanism; what it's for, how it works, when it touches you.
- **The glossary** (`Context/Systems/glossary.md`): a one-line definition for any term you hit.
