## The one-sentence version

This is a folder of plain text notes that an AI helps you write, organize, and maintain, and Obsidian is the app you read it in.

That's the whole idea. Every note is a `.md` file (Markdown, a simple text format) sitting on your own computer. Obsidian shows you the folder; the AI works in the same folder. Anything it writes appears for you instantly, and anything you write is there for it next time.

## What it does for you

Concrete examples of what people use this for:

- **Research that sticks.** Ask the AI to research something and it files the answer as a note you can come back to, instead of losing it in a chat history.
- **Memory across sessions.** At the end of a session the AI writes itself a handoff note. Next time, it picks up where you both left off instead of starting cold.
- **A place to dump messy thoughts.** Drop half-formed ideas in an inbox folder and tell the AI to turn them into organized notes, plans, or project pages.
- **A vault that maintains itself.** The AI keeps its own maps and links tidy, so the folder stays useful as it grows instead of turning into a junk drawer.

## Why plain files matter

**You own your notes.** They're text files on your machine, not locked inside someone's app or cloud. If you stopped using Obsidian or the AI tomorrow, every note would still open in any text editor.

**Nothing is hidden.** What you see in Obsidian is exactly what's on disk. Even the AI's own instructions are readable files you can open.

**It lasts.** Plain text has been readable for decades and will stay readable. Your work isn't tied to any one app's lifespan.

## What you do, what the AI does

You capture ideas and decide what matters. The AI does the heavy lifting: research, drafting, organizing, and keeping track of what's been done. The next page connects the two.
