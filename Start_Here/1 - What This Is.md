## Obsidian in one sentence

Obsidian is a free app that reads a folder of plain text files and lets you write, link, and search across them.

That's the whole idea. This vault is just a folder on your computer. Every note is a `.md` file (Markdown, a simple text format). Open the folder in your file browser and you'll see the same files Obsidian shows you.

## Why that matters

**You own your notes.** They're plain text files on your own machine, not locked inside a company's app or cloud. If you stopped using Obsidian tomorrow, every note would still open in any text editor.

**Nothing is hidden.** What you see is what's on disk. No database, no export step.

**It lasts.** Plain text has been readable for decades and will be readable for decades more. Your work isn't tied to one app's lifespan.

**Your AI can read it too.** Because everything is plain text, an AI assistant can read your notes, follow the links between them, and write new ones, all in the same folder you're working in. That shared workspace is what makes this setup useful.

## What a "vault" is

A vault is just the folder Obsidian has open. This folder is your vault. You can have more than one, but you only need this one to start.

## What you do, what the AI does

You capture ideas and decide what matters. The AI does the heavy lifting: research, drafting, organizing, and keeping track of what's been done. The pages ahead show how to connect the two.
