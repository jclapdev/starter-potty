You know what this is, how Obsidian works, and how to connect your AI. Three things left.

## Step 1: Fill in `main.md`

Open `main.md` at the top of the vault. It's the AI's main instruction file. Fill in the parts marked with `<!-- ... -->` comments:

- **Who I Am**: a sentence or two about your role and how you like to work. The AI uses this to set its tone.
- **Language**: any preference not already listed, for example "always use British English."

Everything else is ready as written. You can change any rule later; it's just text.

## Step 2: Run your first session

With your AI connected (see [[5 - Connect Claude]]), say:

```
read your instructions
```

The AI reads `main.md`, gets its bearings, and tells you what it can do. Then just describe what you want to work on.

## Step 3: Know the rhythm of a session

**Starting:** the AI reads its most recent handoff note and tells you what was left open last time, so you pick up where you stopped.

**During:** the AI follows the rules in `main.md` and uses its skills when they fit.

**Ending:** say **`wrap up`**. The AI reviews the session, updates its notes, and writes a handoff so next time starts warm.

## Where to put new ideas

Anytime you have an idea, drop it in the `Workshop-Human/` folder and tell the AI to process it. You write messy; it turns the mess into real work. (Open that folder's `HUMAN.md` for the details.)

## When you repeat a task often

Tell the AI: *"I want to turn this into a skill."* It will build a reusable process and save it so the task gets easier every time.
