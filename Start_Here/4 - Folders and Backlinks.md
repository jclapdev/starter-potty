## How this vault is organized

Two files at the top level are the AI's instructions: **`main.md`** (your rules and preferences) and **`CLAUDE.md`** (a short pointer that sends Claude to `main.md`). You'll fill in `main.md` on the [[6 - Next Steps]] page.

The folders each have a job:

| Folder | What lives here |
|---|---|
| `Context/` | The AI's brain: its skills, memory, history, and the maps that tie them together |
| `Workshop-Human/` | Your inbox. Jot ideas here and point the AI at them |
| `Projects/` | A note per project you're working on |
| `Resources/` | Reference material you want to keep |
| `Input/` | Drop files here for the AI to read or process |
| `Attachments/` | Images and files embedded inside your notes |
| `AI-Workshop/` | The AI's own working area for outputs and scheduled jobs |

**You don't have to memorize this.** Every folder contains a file named `HUMAN.md` that explains what the folder is for and why it's there. Open any folder, read its `HUMAN.md`, and you'll know what belongs in it.

## How backlinks work

A **link** connects one note to another. In Obsidian you make one by typing `[[` and choosing a note, which gives you something like `[[Home]]`. Click it and you jump there. (The shortcut `Mod + Alt + L` inserts one for you.)

A **backlink** is the same connection seen from the other side. If this note links to `[[Home]]`, then on the Home note you'll see *this* note listed as a backlink: a note pointing back at it.

Why this is useful: you never have to remember what connects to what. Open any note and the backlinks panel (right sidebar) shows every note that mentions or links to it. Connections you made months ago resurface on their own.

To see backlinks: open a note, then open the right sidebar (`Mod + →`) and look at the Backlinks panel.

Official references:
- Internal links: https://help.obsidian.md/links
- Backlinks: https://help.obsidian.md/plugins/backlinks
