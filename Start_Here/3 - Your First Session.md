Claude is connected. This page is the script for your first ten minutes.

## First: tell it who you are

Open `main.md` at the top of the vault. It's the AI's personal-settings file, and two spots are waiting for you, marked with `[square brackets]`:

- **Who I Am**: your name, what you do, and what you want this vault for. A few sentences is plenty. The AI reads this every session.
- **Project-Specific Overrides**: optional; skip it for now.

Everything else is ready as written, and any rule is just text you can change later.

## Then: say the magic words

In the chat, type:

```
read your instructions
```

On a brand-new vault the AI recognizes there's no history yet, confirms setup worked, and offers you a starting point. On every session after this one, the same words make it read its handoff notes and tell you what was left open, so you always pick up where you stopped.

## Try one of these

In your own words or exactly as written:

- "Research [a topic you care about] and file what you find as a note I can come back to."
- "Here's what I'm working on right now: [two or three things]. Make a project note for each and tell me what you'd tackle first."
- "Ask me five questions about how I work, then save what you learn so you remember it."

Watch Obsidian's left sidebar while it works: the notes it creates appear there as real files, because it's all one folder.

## End with a wrap-up

When you're done for the day, say:

```
wrap up
```

The AI reviews the session, updates its notes and maps, and writes a handoff for next time. This is the habit that makes the whole system get better over time: every session starts warm because the last one ended clean.
